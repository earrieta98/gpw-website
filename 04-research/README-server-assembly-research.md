## Key Findings Summary

### Facility Size
- **Total:** 15,000–25,000 sq ft
- **Core production:** 6,000–10,000 sq ft
- **Burn-in area:** 2,000–3,500 sq ft (critical for server assembly)

### Staffing
- **Single shift (8 hours):** 38–40 FTE
- **24-hour operations:** 90–120 FTE (3 shifts × 2.5 multiplier)

### Power Requirements
- **Average continuous:** 200–330 kW
- **Peak simultaneous burn-in:** 400–450 kW
- **Burn-in load alone:** 100–225 kW (cooling + servers)

### Production Throughput
- **Target 5–20 racks/day**
- **Assembly cycle time:** 8–12 hours per rack (complex configurations)
- **Burn-in duration:** 48–72 hours (stress testing)
- **Total time from intake to shipment:** 60–100 hours (5–6 days)

### Critical Zones
1. **Burn-In & Test Area:** Most critical for reliability verification
   - Hot/cold aisle configuration required
   - 48–72 hour continuous operation capability
   - Novec 1230 fire suppression (clean agent, safe for electronics)
   - Precision environmental control (±1°C temperature stability)

2. **Main Assembly (U-Shaped Line):** Optimal layout for 5–20 racks/day
   - 8–15 workstations in U-configuration
   - ESD-compliant flooring and grounding throughout
   - Minimizes walking, maximizes visibility
   - Supports one-piece (one-rack) flow principle

3. **ESD Compliance:** Required in assembly areas per ANSI/ESD S20.20
   - Dissipative/conductive flooring (10⁶–10⁹ ohms)
   - 100% personnel certification required
   - Central ground bus <1 Ω to facility main ground
   - Quarterly flooring resistance testing

### HVAC Requirements
- **Temperature:** 18–27°C (64–81°F), ±1°C stability in burn-in
- **Humidity:** 40–60% RH (critical for component reliability)
- **Cooling load:** 100–150 W/sq ft in burn-in area
- **Air changes:** 6–12 ACH depending on zone

## Implementation Phasing (Months 1–12)

### Phase 1: Core Assembly (Months 1–3)
- Establish receiving, kitting, assembly line
- Target: 2–5 racks/day
- Key milestone: First complete rack assembly

### Phase 2: Test & Validation (Months 3–6)
- Build burn-in facility
- Establish testing protocols
- Target: 5–10 racks/day with full burn-in coverage

### Phase 3: Packaging & Logistics (Months 6–9)
- Shipping dock, crating, inventory management
- Target: 10–15 racks/day

### Phase 4: Scaling & Optimization (Months 9–12)
- Scale to full capacity (20 racks/day)
- Optimize workflows
- Target: Sustainable 20+ racks/day production

## Compliance & Standards

All recommendations align with:
- **ANSI/ESD S20.20-2021:** Electrostatic discharge control
- **ASHRAE TC9.9:** Data center thermal guidelines
- **NFPA 2001 / ISO 14520:** Clean agent fire suppression (Novec 1230)
- **NEC (National Electrical Code):** Electrical installation
- **IPC-A-620:** Cable and wire harness workmanship
- **TIA-942:** Data center infrastructure
- **OSHA:** Worker safety and occupational health

## Monterrey-Specific Considerations

### Climate Profile
- **Temperature:** 18–28°C year-round (favorable for assembly)
- **Humidity:** 50–75% (often humid, especially May–October rainy season)
- **Implications:**
  - Dehumidification required (especially rainy season)
  - Chiller sized appropriately
  - Preventive maintenance for HVAC (frequent filter changes)
  - Equipment rated for tropical/subtropical environment

### Local Advantages
- Reasonable year-round temperatures (aligned with ASHRAE specs)
- Moderate cooling load (less extreme than hot climates)
- Labor availability and cost
- Proximity to US OEM customers (nearshoring advantage)

## Next Steps

1. **Site Selection & Architectural Planning**
   - Use facility footprint (15,000–25,000 sq ft) to identify suitable site
   - Confirm utility capacity (480V service, chilled water if facility chiller planned)
   - Verify dock access and loading area clearance

2. **Detailed Engineering**
   - Engage HVAC engineer to size chiller, in-row coolers, ductwork
   - Electrical engineer: Power distribution, UPS, generator sizing
   - Structural engineer: Flooring, racking, equipment load calculations

3. **Equipment Procurement**
   - Use zone-by-zone equipment lists from checklist document
   - Lead times: Chiller (12–16 weeks), tooling (8–12 weeks), HVAC (8–10 weeks)
   - ESD flooring: Plan installation early (critical path item)

4. **Personnel Recruitment & Training**
   - Begin recruitment 2–3 months before operations start
   - Training program: Minimum 2–3 weeks before launch
   - ESD certification: Mandatory before first assembly work

5. **Process Documentation**
   - Develop detailed work instructions for each assembly step
   - Create QA checklists and test procedures
   - Document burn-in test profiles and acceptance criteria

6. **Pilot Production**
   - Build first 5–10 racks in phases (one zone at a time)
   - Identify procedure gaps and equipment issues
   - Refine processes before full ramp-up
