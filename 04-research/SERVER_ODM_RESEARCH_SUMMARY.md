## Manufacturing Standards (L1–L11)

### The 11 Levels
1. **L1–L3:** Component sourcing & PCBA assembly
2. **L4–L5:** Board testing & node assembly
3. **L6–L7:** System integration & rack assembly
4. **L8–L9:** Burn-in & functional testing
5. **L10–L11:** Packaging & deployment

**Key Insight:** Vertical control of L2–L9 is success criterion for hyperscale manufacturing.

---

## Facility Operations Benchmarks

### Assembly Line Layout
1. **Receiving/Kitting** → 2. **Node Assembly** → 3. **Burn-in/Testing** → 4. **Rack Integration** → 5. **Final QC/Packaging**

### Burn-In Specifications
- **Standard Conditions:** 85°C @ 85% RH for 2–7 days
- **Stress Testing:** 125°C–175°C for semiconductor validation
- **Chamber Power:** AC 380V; facility-wide 500W–5000W per rack
- **Capacity:** 20–60 racks simultaneous (Supermicro = 60 racks)

### Automation Technologies
- **AGVs:** Transport fully-loaded racks (Supermicro Building 21 model)
- **Robotic Assembly:** Machine vision inspection, programmable torque logging
- **Conveyor Systems:** Linear or rotary table, central software coordination
- **Throughput:** 2–5 minute cycle per node; 35 days for 32K-unit hyperscale ramp

## Cable Management & Integration

**Best Practices (Industry Standard):**
- Separate data/power trays; perpendicular crossing
- Velcro straps (not plastic ties) for reconfiguration ease
- Custom harnesses per customer spec
- Full traceability: label both ends, QR codes, documentation
- Reverse cabling method: terminate & test first, then cable final configuration
