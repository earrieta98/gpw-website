## Executive Positioning Statement

Global Precision Works (GPW) enters the electromechanical assembly market at a critical inflection point: major ODMs (Supermicro, Wistron, Foxconn, Quanta) are investing billions in U.S. and Mexico nearshore capacity to meet hyperscaler demand. Rather than compete on raw scale (Supermicro's 600 racks/month), GPW's Monterrey location, assembly-first positioning, and multi-industry focus create a defensible differentiation strategy for Series A growth.

## GPW's Four Differentiation Pillars

### 1. Geography: Monterrey as the Nearshore Hub

**ODM Situation:**
- Supermicro: San Jose (2M sq ft, 2,000 employees) — expensive, saturated
- Wistron: Texas (Dallas/Houston/Fort Worth) — supply chain emerging, 1–2 year lead time
- Foxconn: Taiwan + Mexico + Texas — global but slow deployment
- Celestica: Monterrey exists but not aggressively marketing to hyperscalers

**GPW Advantage:**
- **12-hour delivery to Texas hyperscaler data centers** (vs. 18+ hours from San Jose)
- **Mexico cost structure:** 30–40% labor cost savings vs. U.S.
- **Established precedent:** Celestica's 25-year Monterrey operation proves capability

**Marketing Message:**
*"Precision assembly where it matters: Monterrey proximity meets North American hyperscaler timelines."*

## Investor Pitch Architecture

### The Problem (Market Context)
Hyperscalers and OEMs face a "nearshoring gap": traditional ODMs (Supermicro, Quanta, Foxconn) are overbooked (2–3 year lead times), and new U.S. capacity (Wistron, Inventec) is 12–24 months away from full ramp. Mexico has precedent (Celestica) but no modern, dedicated hyperscale EMS player.

**Market Size:** $120B+ EMS industry; AI/hyperscale subset = $8–12B annually and growing 40%+ YoY.

### The Solution (GPW Positioning)
GPW fills the nearshore gap with a "dedicated assembly plant for hyperscaler AND industrial OEM overflow." Monterrey location, assembly-first model, and 8-industry footprint create defensible competitive moat until GPW itself reaches 300+ racks/month.

### The Proof (Celestica Precedent)
Celestica has operated in Monterrey since 1998 with IATF certification, proving:
1. Mexico can deliver hyperscale quality
2. Nearshore model works for precision assembly
3. Customer relationships are sticky (long-term contracts)

### The Unit Economics (Year 1 Snapshot)

**Revenue Assumptions (Conservative):**
- 75 racks/month avg (Year 1)
- $150K–$200K blended margin per rack (assembly + integration)
- Mix: 40% hyperscale ($180K margin), 50% industrial ($140K), 10% specialty ($250K)
- **Blended margin:** ~$145K per rack
- **Annual rack revenue:** 75 × 12 = 900 racks × $145K = **$130.5M**

**Cost Assumptions:**
- Facility lease & utilities: $2M/year (large, modern facility)
- Equipment & tooling: $5M (depreciated)
- Labor (100–120 people @ $400K per headcount blended): $40–48M
- Materials & SG&A: $15M
- **Total OpEx:** ~$65–70M

**Gross Margin:** ($130.5M − $65M) / $130.5M = **50% (Year 1)**

**Note:** These are illustrative. Actual numbers depend on customer mix, utilization ramp, and final service pricing. But the structure shows profitability is achievable at 75 racks/month if execution is tight.

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: "Why not just use Celestica or Wistron's Mexico plant?"

**Mitigation:**
- Celestica: Passive presence, legacy customer base, not aggressively pursuing hyperscale
- Wistron: 2–3 year lead time for new Mexico capacity; GPW is operational now
- **Message:** "We're the specialists *for* hyperscalers, not the generalists serving everyone."

### Risk 2: "GPW is too small to meet hyperscaler demand"

**Mitigation:**
- Position as "overflow capacity partner" (100 racks/month is valuable to hyperscalers with 10K+/year demand)
- Build partnerships with other nearshore EMS (Flex, Sanmina) for co-investment or acquisition runway
- **Message:** "Our size is our strength: responsive, flexible, dedicated to your success."

### Risk 3: "Mexico labor costs won't stay low; automation will shift back to U.S."

**Mitigation:**
- Build automation roadmap (AGVs, vision inspection, burn-in at scale) to compete on efficiency, not just labor
- Target 15–20% annual automation investment (Foxconn's model)
- **Message:** "GPW combines Mexico economics with automation parity to ODMs."

### Risk 4: "Hyperscalers will just wait for Wistron/Inventec to ramp"

**Mitigation:**
- Lock in **multi-year contracts** with volume commitments (1–5 year GPW+X hyperscaler partnership)
- Offer **volume discounts** for predictable, long-term demand (reduces their scheduling risk)
- **Message:** "Partnership over transaction: We grow together, or we don't grow."
