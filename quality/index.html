CNC Machining · Network Quality Governance

How GPW governs quality without owning the machines.

GPW produces machined parts through a vetted manufacturing network of Monterrey machine shops. GPW does not operate the machines — and it does own the quality outcome. The network is qualified, audited, and held to GPW's inspection and documentation standard on every job. Capacity scales across shops; the quality bar does not move.

Supplier Qualification
GPW vets shops on capability, equipment, and quality record before they receive work; performance is monitored and re-evaluated as work runs.In place
Incoming Material Inspection
GPW checks raw stock against the order — alloy, grade, temper, dimensions — before machining begins.In place
First-Article Inspection (FAI)
Before a production run, GPW inspects the first part against every drawing dimension and signs it off. A PPAP-style first-article inspection report is available on request.In place
In-Process & Final Inspection
GPW runs dimensional checks during the run and a final inspection against the drawing before parts ship. Inspection level scales to the part's criticality.In place
Certificate of Conformance
GPW can include a signed CoC with every shipment, stating the parts were produced and inspected to your drawing and specification.In place
Material Certs / Mill Reports
GPW traces mill certificates and material test reports to the lot and ships them with the parts when required.In place
Lot Traceability
Parts are traceable by lot back to material and shop — so a question on a delivered part can be answered, not guessed.In place
What GPW does not claim: owned machining equipment, machine counts, or a GPW shop floor. The machines belong to the network shops. GPW owns the engineering, the inspection regime, the documentation, and the accountability — the first quality gate is a free in-house DFM review before any shop opens your file.

How We Document Quality

Every build, documented against criteria.

Quality at GPW is built into the workflow, not added at the end. Here is how each order moves through documented inspection and record-keeping — so what ships is verifiable against what was ordered, every time.

01 Before the run

Documented Plan

Documented work instructions, control plans, and the inspection plan are defined per part number across machining governance and in-house assembly.

02 First part

First-Article Inspection

The first part is inspected against every drawing dimension and signed off before a production run begins — recorded in a first-article inspection report.

03 During the run

In-Process Checks

Dimensional and workmanship checks at defined stages against documented criteria — so a defect is caught where it happens, not at final test.

04 At ship

Documentation & Traceability

Final QA, a signed Certificate of Conformance, material certs, and full lot traceability — audit-ready documentation that matches what was ordered.

Built for the programs we serve. Across aerospace, medical, automotive, and the rest, GPW builds to the documentation, traceability, and inspection requirements each customer program defines — with audit-ready records to back it.
FAQ

Quality & compliance — answered.

How does GPW manage quality?

Every build follows documented workmanship and inspection standards, with first-article inspection, in-process checks, and full traceability. Document control and lot traceability are in place now, and we align to the quality requirements each customer program defines.

How can you guarantee quality if you don't own the machines?

GPW owns the quality outcome, not the machines. The Monterrey machining network is qualified and audited, and every job runs to GPW's inspection and documentation standard: incoming inspection, first-article inspection, and a Certificate of Conformance. One accountable owner, scalable capacity — never a broker hand-off.

What documentation ships with my parts?

On request: a signed Certificate of Conformance, material certifications and mill test reports traced to the lot, and a first-article inspection report. Tell us your requirements with the RFQ and we confirm the documentation package before production begins.

Do you do first-article inspection (FAI)?

Yes. Before a production run, the first part is inspected against every drawing dimension and signed off. A PPAP-style first-article inspection report is available when your program requires it.

What workmanship standards do you build assemblies to?

In-house assemblies are built against documented workmanship and inspection criteria for electronic assemblies and for cable and wire harnesses, with first-article inspection and in-process checks. We align to the workmanship requirements each customer program defines.

Can you support aerospace or medical traceability requirements?

Yes. GPW operates full lot traceability and documented inspection today, with audit-ready documentation. We build to the documentation, traceability, and inspection requirements your aerospace or medical program defines.

What does GPW's quality system cover today?

Documented work instructions and document control, the network inspection regime, first-article and in-process inspection, the Certificate of Conformance, full lot traceability, and in-house assembly test — all against documented criteria. We align to the quality requirements each customer program defines.
