Heat sink fins. Enclosure walls. EMI grooves.

Aluminum 6063 for heat sinks (best anodize quality, 201 W/m·K thermal conductivity). Aluminum 6061 for structural enclosures. Copper 101 for maximum thermal performance. Heat-sink base flatness held to 0.001"–0.002". Fin pitch optimized for thermal vs machining tradeoff during DFM.

Step 02 · Finish for Function

Anodize for thermal. Chem film for EMI.

Anodize Type II on heat sinks — emissivity 0.85 vs 0.05 for bare aluminum, 3–5× better radiation. Chem film (Alodine) on grounding surfaces — conductive, MIL-DTL-5541. Nickel plate on EMI shielding. Powder coat on cosmetic exteriors. Right finish for the right surface, advised during DFM.

FAQ

Electronics machining — answered.

Can you machine complex heat sink geometries?

Yes — pin-fin, straight-fin, cross-cut, and custom profiles. We optimize fin geometry during DFM review — balancing thermal performance against machining feasibility and cost. Sometimes widening the fin pitch by 0.5 mm reduces machining time 30% with less than 2°C thermal impact.

Do you do both the machining and the assembly?

Yes. Our Contract Manufacturing division machines the enclosures, heat sinks, chassis, and precision components. Our EMS Assembly division mounts boards, routes cables, loads firmware, tests, and ships the finished product. One supplier, one accountability chain. → EMS Assembly

What about EMI shielding effectiveness?

Machined aluminum or stainless enclosures with conductive finishes (chem film, nickel plate) and gasket grooves provide excellent EMI/RFI shielding. We advise on material, finish, and gasket groove design during DFM review.

Can you build telecom cabinets and rack-mount equipment?

Yes — sheet metal for the cabinet structure, CNC machining for precision components (brackets, rails, heat sinks), and EMS assembly for the full system integration. → Sheet Metal

What about small batches for new product development?

No minimum order. Prototype quantities (1–25 pieces) in production materials with full dimensional inspection. Ideal for hardware engineering teams iterating on enclosure designs.

Do you handle RoHS compliance?

Yes. All materials and finishes comply with RoHS directives. No lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, PBB, or PBDE. Compliance documentation available upon request.
