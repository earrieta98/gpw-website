For an Accurate Quote

What Helps Us Quote Accurately

The more information you provide, the more accurate your quote will be. Don't have all of this? Submit what you have — we will ask for what is missing.

Preferred

CAD File (STEP)

Gives us exact geometry with no ambiguity. STEP is the universal 3D format and our preferred starting point.

Critical

2D Drawing with GD&T

Critical for tolerance callouts, surface finish specs, and thread details. The drawing tells us what matters most on each feature.

Specific

Material Spec

Alloy and temper (e.g., "Aluminum 6061-T6", not just "aluminum"). For exotic alloys, include the ASTM/AMS spec.

If Required

Surface Finish Requirements

As-machined, anodized, plated, or specific Ra value. Surface finish affects cost, lead time, and supplier selection.

Recurring vs One-Off

Quantity + Frequency

One-time order or recurring production? Annual volume estimate helps us optimize pricing — blanket POs unlock better per-piece cost.

Context

Industry / Application

Tells us which certifications and documentation to include — PPAP for automotive, AS9102 for aerospace, MTR + NACE for oil & gas.

Don't have all of this? Submit what you have. A sketch with dimensions works. A photo of an existing part works. A description of what you need works. We will ask for what is missing — we will not reject your request because it is incomplete.
