## 1. Executive Summary

This Content Strategy document defines the narrative architecture, production roadmap, and execution framework for all 31 pages across the GPW Solutions website. It serves as the bridge between Phase 1 research (competitive analysis, keyword research, site architecture) and Phase 3 production (copywriting and HTML prototypes).

### Strategic Purpose
The content strategy ensures that every page on gpw-solutions.com:
1. Answers a specific customer question at a defined buyer stage
2. Targets high-intent keywords identified in keyword research
3. Reinforces one of five content pillars that organize GPW's competitive advantage
4. Drives customers toward the primary conversion goal (RFQ submission)
5. Maintains consistent brand voice and positioning across all industries

### Key Metrics at a Glance
- **Total Pages:** 31 (organized across 7 content sections)
- **Content Pillars:** 5 strategic narratives
- **Industries Served:** 8 verticals with dedicated pages
- **Services Highlighted:** 5 core capabilities
- **Buyer Personas:** 4 decision-maker types (VP Supply Chain, Procurement Director, Engineering Lead, Operations Director)
- **Target Customer Journey:** Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Conversion (RFQ)
- **Production Timeline:** 16 weeks (6 sprints, 1 buffer week)
- **Primary CTA:** Request for Quote (RFQ) submission
- **Secondary CTAs:** Download capabilities brochure, request facility tour, schedule discovery call

### How This Document Feeds Phase 3
Phase 3 (Content Creation) will consume this strategy through:
- **Topic clusters** (Section 4) that define which keywords each page targets
- **Page-by-page briefs** (Section 6) that specify content length, CTAs, and messaging priorities
- **Production priority** (Section 7) that sequences copywriting assignments
- **Content guidelines** (Section 12) that standardize brand voice, on-page SEO, and CTA placement
- **Internal linking rules** (Section 11) that guide content team on cross-page connections

## 3. Content Pillars

GPW's competitive advantage is organized into five content pillars. Every page on the site will reinforce at least one pillar; most pages reinforce two or three. These pillars structure the entire content strategy and guide copywriting decisions.

### Pillar 1: Nearshore Manufacturing Advantage
**Theme:** Why Mexico, why Monterrey, why now — framing nearshoring as superior to both domestic U.S. manufacturing and Asian outsourcing.

**Core Message:** Monterrey offers the optimal balance of cost, speed, quality, and supply chain resilience. Two hours from the U.S. border, same timezone, USMCA-compliant, and staffed with skilled labor. Tariff-advantaged vs. Asian sourcing. Faster NPI than domestic. More agile than mega-factory operations.

**Pages:**
- Corporate Home (hero message)
- EMS Division Home (sub-hero)
- About EMS (company story, facility detail)
- Blog: Nearshoring Guide (SEO landing page)
- Blog: Mexico Manufacturing Advantages (thought leadership)
- Blog: USMCA Benefits for OEMs (tariff education)

**Primary Keywords:**
- nearshoring Mexico, nearshoring manufacturing, nearshoring benefits
- manufacturing in Monterrey, electronics manufacturing Mexico
- USMCA manufacturing, tariff advantages nearshoring
- Mexico contract manufacturing, Mexico electronics assembly

**Search Intent & Buyer Stage:**
- Awareness: "Why nearshore to Mexico?" (informational)
- Consideration: "Is Monterrey better than Asia?" (comparative)
- Decision: "Which nearshore partner in Mexico?" (commercial)

**Tone:**
- Confident without defensive posturing
- Data-driven (tariff savings, delivery speed, USMCA facts)
- Customer-centric (addresses supply chain resilience concerns)

## 5. Content Types Strategy

GPW's content will be distributed across three primary content types, each serving a distinct purpose in the buyer's journey.

### Distribution: 70% Searchable, 20% Shareable, 10% Conversion

#### 70% Searchable Content (SEO-First, Owned Audience)
**Definition:** Content optimized for organic search ranking, designed to capture high-intent keywords and drive qualified traffic.

**Examples:**
- Service pages (5): Box Build Assembly, Cable Harness, System Integration, Testing, Enclosure
- Industry pages (8): AI, Industrial, Telecom, Medical, Automotive, Energy, Aerospace, Appliances
- Hub pages (3): Services, Blog, EMS Home
- Comparison pages (3): Nearshore vs. Offshore, Mid-Market vs. Mega-EMS, Agile vs. Traditional
- SEO blog posts (6): Nearshoring Guide, Mexico Advantages, USMCA Benefits, Box Build Guide, How to Choose CM, IPC Standards & DFM

**Strategy:**
- Optimize for commercial and decision-stage keywords
- Target 2,000-4,000 words for service and industry pages
- Structure with H2s answering specific search intents
- Include definition blocks for "What is X?" queries
- Add FAQ sections addressing common objections
- Include comparison tables for "X vs Y" queries
- Natural internal linking to pillar pages and other services/industries
- Schema markup for LocalBusiness, Article, FAQPage types

**Measurement:**
- Monthly organic traffic per page
- Search Console impressions and click-through rate
- Average position for target keywords
- Page scroll depth and time on page

---

#### 20% Shareable Content (Thought Leadership, Social/Email)
**Definition:** Content designed to build brand authority, generate links, and be shared by customers/partners across their networks.

**Examples:**
- Blog: Mexico Manufacturing Advantages (original research angle)
- Blog: AI Hardware Manufacturing Trends (industry insight)
- Blog: Rising Cost of Asian Outsourcing (macro data)
- About EMS (facility story, team depth)
- Corporate Home (brand positioning)

**Strategy:**
- Lead with original insights, proprietary data, or customer stories
- Use statistics and trends that OEMs will want to reference
- Create infographics or downloadable resources (e.g., "Nearshoring Checklist," "RFQ Template")
- Quote industry analysts, cite research institutions
- Shareable angles: tariff savings calculators, supply chain risk assessments, ROI models
- Position GPW as thought leader in nearshoring, not just another EMS vendor

**Measurement:**
- Social shares, email forwards, inbound links
- Brand mentions in industry publications
- Engagement metrics (comments, shares)
- Brand search volume growth

---

#### 10% Conversion Content (CTAs, Forms, Direct Response)
**Definition:** Content designed to move prospects toward immediate action: RFQ submission, brochure download, facility tour request, discovery call.

**Examples:**
- Request Quote page (primary CTA)
- Capabilities brochure (downloadable, email-gated)
- Facility tour request form
- Discovery call booking
- Contact page
- Corporate Contact page
- Bottom-of-funnel comparison pages (Nearshore vs. Offshore, Mid-Market vs. Mega-EMS, Agile vs. Traditional)

**Strategy:**
- Remove friction: clear value proposition, 2-3 form fields max
- Multiple CTA pathways: "Get RFQ," "Download Brochure," "Schedule Tour"
- Reassurance elements: response time SLA, team credentials
- Dynamic CTAs based on industry/service context (JavaScript-driven)
- Email follow-up sequences post-submission (set up in WordPress/email automation)
- Retargeting: show comparison pages and case studies to repeat visitors

**Measurement:**
- Form submission rate
- CTA click-through rate by page type
- Lead quality (responded to, converted to customer)
- Cost per qualified lead

## 10. AI SEO Integration Points

This section identifies where AI-generated SEO enhancements will integrate with the content created in Phase 3. The AI SEO strategy (separate document, Phase 2) will define implementation details; this section maps integration points.

### 10.1 Definition Blocks ("What is X?" Queries)

**Where:** Every service page and industry page + blog posts.
**What:** 50-100 word definition blocks answering "What is [service]?" or "What is [industry] assembly?"
**Why:** Captures informational search intent, positions GPW as authority, supports featured snippet ranking.

**Examples:**
- Service page hero or opening section: "What is box build assembly?" (definition + relevance to GPW)
- Industry page opening: "What is AI hardware assembly?" (definition + industry context)
- Blog post: "What is electromechanical assembly?" (definitional blog post can rank for high-volume educational queries)

**AI SEO Enhancement:** AI tool will generate definition blocks optimized for featured snippet position (40-60 words, answering the question directly, Q&A format option).

**Phase 4 Implementation:** Embed in page template, optimize for schema (FAQPage, Definition, etc.)

---

### 10.2 FAQ Sections (Anticipate Customer Questions)

**Where:** All service pages (5) + all industry pages (8) + selected blog posts (5).
**What:** 5-10 FAQ items per page, answering common customer questions, structured for FAQ schema.
**Why:** Captures long-tail keyword variations, addresses buyer objections, supports rich snippet display in search results.

**Examples:**

**Service Page FAQ (Box Build Assembly):**
- "How long does box build assembly take?" (Answer: "Typical 4-12 week timeline...")
- "What certifications does GPW have for box build assembly?" (Answer: "IPC J-STD-001...")
- "Can you handle rapid design changes during production?" (Answer: "Yes, our agile process...")

**Industry Page FAQ (Medical Devices):**
- "Are you ISO 13485 certified?" (Answer: "We are actively pursuing ISO 13485 certification. Our processes are designed to meet ISO 13485 standards.")
- "Do you support design history files?" (Answer: "Complete DHF support...")
- "What's your experience with FDA compliance?" (Answer: "15+ years...")

**Blog Post FAQ (How to Choose CM):**
- "What should I ask a contract manufacturer about lead times?" (Answer: "Confirm baseline, rush capacity...")
- "How do I verify a CM's quality claims?" (Answer: "Request certifications, third-party audits...")

**AI SEO Enhancement:** AI tool will generate FAQ items based on:
1. Keyword research (search volume for "question" variations)
2. Competitor FAQ audit (what others ask)
3. Customer research (actual questions from sales/support)

**Phase 4 Implementation:** FAQ schema markup (FAQPage) for search result display, collapsible UI in WordPress.

## 11. Internal Linking Strategy Summary

This section provides the internal linking framework that will be applied during Phase 3 copywriting and Phase 4 implementation.

### Core Linking Principles

1. **Hub & Spoke Model:** Services Hub and Industry Hub are central hubs; all detail pages (service pages, industry pages, blogs) link to and from hubs.

2. **Contextual Relevance:** Links are placed where they make sense for the reader, not forced keyword optimization. Anchor text is descriptive, not generic ("click here," "learn more").

3. **Customer Journey Flow:**
   - Blog (Awareness) → Services (Consideration) → Industry (Decision) → RFQ (Conversion)
   - Or: Home → EMS Home → Services Hub → Service detail → Industry detail → RFQ

4. **Authority Distribution:** Link older, high-authority pages (home, hubs) to newer pages to accelerate their ranking.

5. **Minimum Linking Standard:** Every page has at least 2-3 internal links to other relevant pages (no orphan pages).

---

### Linking Rules by Page Type

#### Service Pages (5 pages)
**Outbound Links:**
- Link to: All 8 industry pages (anchor: "[Industry] assembly" or "Learn how GPW serves [Industry]")
- Link to: Other service pages (anchor: "complementary service" or "next step")
- Link to: Related blog post (anchor: "[Service] Guide" or "industry best practices")
- Link to: About/Testing/Quality section (anchor: "our quality standards")
- Link to: Request Quote (anchor: "ready to start your project?")

**Total outbound links per page:** 8-12

**Example (Box Build Assembly page):**

## 12. Content Guidelines Quick Reference

This section provides copywriters with a fast-reference guide to brand voice, on-page SEO standards, and CTA placement rules.

### 12.1 Brand Voice Guidelines

**GPW's Voice:**
- **Tone:** Professional, confident, technical, direct
- **Persona:** Expert advisor, not hard-sell marketer
- **Key phrases:** "engineered for," "ready to scale," "built to deliver," "precision," "agility," "partnership"

**Word bank (USE THESE):**
- Precision, rigorous, specification, integration, optimize, reliable, scalable, flexible, nearshore, capable, expertise, partner, solution, streamline, advance

**Word bank (AVOID THESE):**
- "Best," "world-class" (superlatives without proof), "maybe," "might," "perhaps" (wishy-washy), "revolutionary," "disruptive" (hype)

**Tone examples:**

**GOOD (Confident, technical, direct):**
- "Our box build assembly process handles mechanical + electrical + cabling + testing in one integrated workflow."
- "Medical device assembly requires ISO 13485-level discipline and rigorous traceability. We deliver both."
- "Nearshoring to Monterrey cuts lead times 40% vs. Asian sourcing and eliminates tariff costs."

**BAD (Hype, wishy-washy, generic):**
- "We're the world's best box build manufacturer!" (Unproven, superlative)
- "Medical device assembly might need certification." (Wishy-washy)
- "Our innovative, disruptive approach to nearshoring is revolutionary." (Hype, vague)

---

### 12.2 Word Count Targets by Page Type

| Page Type | Word Count | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Homepage | 1,500-1,800 | Scannable, multiple CTAs, not dense |
| Service page (detailed) | 3,000-3,500 | SEO depth, multiple H2s, FAQ section, internal links |
| Industry page | 3,000-4,000 | Vertical expertise, regulatory/standards, case study setup |
| Blog post (SEO) | 2,000-3,000 | Keyword targeting, H2 structure, FAQ section |
| Blog post (thought leadership) | 2,000-2,500 | Readable, shareable, not encyclopedic |
| Comparison page | 2,500-3,000 | Detailed comparison tables, not pure copy |
| About page | 2,500-3,000 | Company narrative, team, credentials, credibility signals |
| Hub page (Services, Blog) | 2,500-3,000 | Navigation, discovery, filter/sort features |

**Note:** Word counts are targets, not minimums. If you have more to say with value, write more. If you can say it in 2,500 words and it's tight, that's fine.

### 12.3 CTA Placement Rules

**Above the Fold (first 500px):**
- Primary CTA banner or button visible on scroll
- Recommendation: "Request a Quote" or "Explore Our Services"
- Placement: Top-right nav, or hero section overlay

**Mid-Page (around 50% scroll):**
- Secondary CTA after main value proposition
- Recommendation: Industry/service-specific: "Learn about [service] for [industry]" or "Download [guide]"
- Placement: After key benefit section, inline or sidebar

**Bottom of Page (before footer):**
- Tertiary CTA, alternative conversion path
- Recommendation: "Schedule a facility tour," "Talk to our team," or "Explore related services"
- Placement: Call-to-action section before footer

**Multiple CTAs per page (OK):**
- Service/industry pages: 2-3 CTAs (e.g., RFQ + Download + Tour request)
- Blog posts: 1-2 CTAs (main: RFQ, secondary: related blog or service link)
- Hub pages: 2 CTAs (browse + RFQ)

**CTA Button Color:**
- Primary CTA: Coral #ED835E (brand accent, calls attention)
- Secondary CTA: Teal #23555A (softer, brand primary)
- Tertiary CTA: Text link + icon (minimal, contextual)

### 12.4 On-Page SEO Checklist

**For Every Page:**
- [ ] Title tag: 50-60 characters, includes primary keyword + brand
- [ ] Meta description: 155-160 characters, answers the search query, includes CTA language
- [ ] H1: One per page, exact or close to title tag, includes primary keyword naturally
- [ ] H2s: 3-5 per page, answer common search intents/questions, include secondary keywords where natural
- [ ] H3s: Under H2s, support sub-topics, natural keyword inclusion
- [ ] Images: At least 1 per 500 words, optimized alt text (keyword-aware but descriptive)
- [ ] Internal links: 2-3 minimum per page, contextual anchor text, relevant pages
- [ ] External links: 1-2 authoritative sources (if content cites data)
- [ ] Readability: Varied sentence length, short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), bullet points for lists
- [ ] Mobile optimization: Tested on mobile, readable text size, clear CTAs
- [ ] Page speed: Images compressed, no auto-playing video, minimal plugins
- [ ] Schema markup: Organization + relevant schema (Service, Article, FAQ, etc.)

**Content-Specific Checks:**

**Service Pages:**
- [ ] Service definition (what is X?) prominently in opening
- [ ] "Why GPW" positioning (what makes us different)
- [ ] Industry relevance indicators (which industries use this service)
- [ ] Capability checklist (capacity, timeline, certifications)
- [ ] FAQ section addressing objections

**Industry Pages:**
- [ ] Industry context (why assembly matters in this vertical)
- [ ] Regulatory/standards landscape
- [ ] Typical projects (case study setup)
- [ ] Services relevant to this industry (cross-linking)
- [ ] Why nearshoring to Mexico matters for this industry

**Blog Posts:**
- [ ] Intro paragraph hooks the reader (problem statement or surprising fact)
- [ ] Body section addresses search intent (educational, comparative, trend)
- [ ] Conclusion summarizes key takeaway
- [ ] CTA is natural (not forced), drives to service page or RFQ
