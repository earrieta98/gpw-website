How to read this page

Two labels. No gray areas.

Risk-averse buyers get burned by suppliers who blur capability and credential. GPW does not. Every quality control on this page carries one of two labels — so you always know what is operating today and what is a dated commitment.

In place

A process or document control built into GPW's standard workflow and applied on every order GPW takes — operational and ready, not a future promise.

In progress

A formal, third-party certification GPW is working toward, with a target date. We name the date — we do not claim the certificate until a registrar issues it.

You will not find a certification logo on this page that GPW does not hold. When GPW earns one, it moves from in progress to in place — with the audit date.

CNC Machining · Network Quality Governance

How GPW governs quality without owning the machines.

GPW produces machined parts through a vetted manufacturing network of Monterrey machine shops. GPW does not operate the machines — and it does own the quality outcome. The network is qualified, audited, and held to GPW's inspection and documentation standard on every job. Capacity scales across shops; the quality bar does not move.

Supplier Qualification
GPW vets shops on capability, equipment, and quality record before they receive work; performance is monitored and re-evaluated as work runs.In place
Incoming Material Inspection
GPW checks raw stock against the order — alloy, grade, temper, dimensions — before machining begins.In place
First-Article Inspection (FAI)
Before a production run, GPW inspects the first part against every drawing dimension and signs it off. An AS9100-format / PPAP-style FAI report is available on request.In place
In-Process & Final Inspection
GPW runs dimensional checks during the run and a final inspection against the drawing before parts ship. Inspection level scales to the part's criticality.In place
Certificate of Conformance
GPW can include a signed CoC with every shipment, stating the parts were produced and inspected to your drawing and specification.In place
Material Certs / Mill Reports
GPW traces mill certificates and material test reports to the lot and ships them with the parts when required.In place
Lot Traceability
Parts are traceable by lot back to material and shop — so a question on a delivered part can be answered, not guessed.In place
What GPW does not claim: owned machining equipment, machine counts, or a GPW shop floor. The machines belong to the network shops. GPW owns the engineering, the inspection regime, the documentation, and the accountability — the first quality gate is a free in-house DFM review before any shop opens your file.

ISO 9001 Roadmap

The path to ISO 9001:2015 — with dates.

GPW is pre-certification and says so. Here is the sequence and the timing. Until a registrar issues the certificate, ISO 9001 stays labeled in progress — no exceptions.

01 H2 2026

Gap Assessment

Map current processes against ISO 9001:2015 and define the documented QMS scope across machining governance and in-house assembly.

02 Q1 2027

Documented QMS Operating

Quality manual, procedures, and records live and in use on real jobs. The system has to run before it can be audited.

03 Q2 2027

Internal Audits & Review

Internal audit cycle and management review completed, corrective actions closed — the evidence a registrar looks for.

04 Q3 2027

Certification Audit

Stage 1 and Stage 2 registrar audit. On a pass, ISO 9001 moves from in progress to in place — with the certificate number and date published here.

Sector certifications follow the base system. AS9100D, ISO 13485, and IATF 16949 are targeted for 2028 onward, in step with the industries GPW is actively serving. Each stays labeled in progress until certified.
FAQ

Quality & compliance — answered.

Is GPW ISO 9001 certified?

Not yet — and we won't say otherwise. GPW's ISO 9001:2015 quality management system is in progress, with the certification audit targeted for Q3 2027. The underlying processes — inspection, document control, traceability — are in place now; the certificate follows the registrar audit.

How can you guarantee quality if you don't own the machines?

GPW owns the quality outcome, not the machines. The Monterrey machining network is qualified and audited, and every job runs to GPW's inspection and documentation standard: incoming inspection, first-article inspection, and a Certificate of Conformance. One accountable owner, scalable capacity — never a broker hand-off.

What documentation ships with my parts?

On request: a signed Certificate of Conformance, material certifications and mill test reports traced to the lot, and a first-article inspection report. Tell us your requirements with the RFQ and we confirm the documentation package before production begins.

Do you do first-article inspection (FAI)?

Yes. Before a production run, the first part is inspected against every drawing dimension and signed off. An AS9100-format / PPAP-style FAI report is available when your program requires it.

What workmanship standards do you build assemblies to?

In-house assemblies are built to IPC-A-610 for electronic assemblies and IPC/WHMA-A-620 for cable and wire harnesses. These are build-to standards today; formal IPC operator certification is on the roadmap, labeled as in progress.

Can you support aerospace or medical traceability requirements?

GPW operates lot traceability and documented inspection today, which covers many aerospace and medical sub-assembly requirements. AS9100D and ISO 13485 certifications are in progress, targeted for 2028 — stated as roadmap, not as certificates already held.

What is in place today versus in progress?

In place: the QMS processes, the network inspection regime, the Certificate of Conformance, lot traceability, and in-house assembly test. In progress: ISO 9001 (2027), then AS9100D, ISO 13485, and IATF 16949 (2028 onward). Every control on this page is labeled.
