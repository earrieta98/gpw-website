The Reality of Automotive

Cost. Volume. Documentation.

Automotive procurement is driven by three forces. Miss the target price, you lose the bid. Cannot scale to volume, you lose the program. PPAP package incomplete, you never get qualified. We are structured around those three from the first PO — not after the program ramps.

Prototype to Production Line

How an automotive program moves through GPW.

Step 01 · Prototype (1–50 pcs)

Functional parts in production materials.

No 3D-printed approximations. Aluminum, steel, brass, stainless — machined the way the production part will be machined. 1–2 week lead times for design validation. Real properties. Real fit. Real function.

Step 02 · Pre-Production (50–500 pcs)

PPAP Level 3. Submitted, not promised.

Control plan. Process FMEA. Dimensional results. Material certs by heat lot. MSA gauge R&R. SPC capability data. Process flow diagram. Appearance approval. The full package — ready to upload to your supplier portal.

Step 03 · Production (500–10,000+/mo)

Scale by adding shifts — not corners.

Same machines, same operators, same process from prototype through ramp. SPC on critical dimensions every shift. Out-of-spec triggers immediate containment. No re-qualification. No surprises at scale.

Throughout · Annual Cost-Down

Value engineering, built in.

OEMs demand cost-down every year. We respond with continuous DFM analysis on your active parts — tolerance relaxation, material substitution, part consolidation, setup reduction. Proactive savings. Not just when you ask.

FAQ

Automotive machining — answered.

Can you handle automotive production volumes?

Yes — from 5-piece prototypes to 10,000+ parts per month. We scale by adding shifts and machines, not by cutting corners. Volume ramps are planned during PPAP so capacity is in place before the program needs it.

Do you provide PPAP documentation?

Yes — full Level 3 PPAP package: control plan, FMEA, dimensional results, material certs, MSA, SPC capability data, process flow diagram, and appearance approval. We know that without PPAP, you cannot ship parts.

How fast can you turn prototypes?

1–2 weeks for standard materials and complexity. Functional prototypes in production materials — aluminum, steel, stainless, brass — not 3D-printed substitutes. Real mechanical properties for real validation testing.

Do you work with Tier 1 suppliers?

Yes. We serve both OEMs and their Tier 1 / Tier 2 supply chain. If your Tier 1 already operates in Mexico, we integrate into your existing logistics network — reducing lead times, shipping costs, and customs complexity.

How do you handle annual cost-down requirements?

Through value engineering — ongoing analysis of your parts for material substitution, tolerance relaxation, part consolidation, and process optimization. We proactively look for savings, not just when you ask. → Engineering Support

Can you combine machined parts with assembly?

Yes. GPW's EMS division handles electromechanical assembly — from cable harness to box build to system integration. → See EMS Assembly
