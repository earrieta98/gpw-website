The Reality of Aerospace

The paperwork is part of the product.

Aerospace is the most demanding machining environment. Tolerances measured in tenths of a thousandth. Materials that fight back. Documentation requirements that rival the complexity of the parts themselves. We are built for this from the first PO — pre-certified, not pre-organized.

From Stock to Flight-Ready

How an aerospace part moves through GPW.

Step 01 · Sourcing & Traceability

Material arrives with its history.

Titanium Grade 5 per ASTM F136. Inconel 718 with MTR. Aluminum 7075-T6 mill-certified. Every bar links to a heat lot before a single chip is cut. No traceability gap, no substitution risk.

Step 02 · 5-Axis Machining

Tolerances in tenths.

A bracket holding ±0.005" in industrial holds ±0.0005" in an aircraft. No excess material to absorb error. Titanium machines 3× harder than steel; Inconel 718 work-hardens on contact. Demands experienced operators and rigid setups.

Step 03 · First Article Inspection

Every dimension. Every characteristic.

First Article per AS9102. Material certifications per AMS / ASTM. Dimensional inspection reports. Certificate of Conformance. Process records identifying machine, operator, date, and tooling. The package is delivered with the parts, not on request.

Step 04 · Delivered to U.S. Aerospace Hubs

USMCA. ITAR-controlled. Fast.

Monterrey to Wichita: 3-hour flight. To Dallas / Fort Worth: 1.5 hr. To Houston: 1.5 hr. Same-day air freight reaches every major U.S. aerospace hub. Country of origin: North America.

FAQ

Aerospace machining — answered.

Do you hold AS9100D certification?

GPW is on the AS9100D certification pathway. Our processes follow AS9100-aligned quality procedures today, and we provide full documentation packages — FAI per AS9102, material certifications, CoC, dimensional reports, and process records — on every aerospace project.

Can you machine titanium and Inconel?

Yes. These materials require experienced operators, rigid machine setups, and proper cutting strategies — slow speeds, high feed, sharp carbide or ceramic tooling, and aggressive coolant. Our partner shops have dedicated setups for exotic alloys.

How do you handle ITAR-controlled parts?

We sign NDAs and ITAR agreements before receiving any files or technical data. Controlled access protocols are in place. Contact us to discuss your specific ITAR requirements before submitting files.

What documentation do you provide for aerospace projects?

Full aerospace documentation package: First Article Inspection (AS9102), material certifications (AMS/ASTM mill certs), dimensional inspection reports, Certificate of Conformance, and process records. Scope is defined per project.

Can you handle both prototype and production aerospace parts?

Yes. From 1-piece prototype for design validation to recurring production runs. Prototype parts are made from the same certified material and inspected to the same standard as production parts.

Do you provide non-destructive testing (NDT)?

Through NADCAP-accredited partner labs: fluorescent penetrant inspection (FPI), magnetic particle inspection (MPI), radiographic inspection, and ultrasonic testing. Coordinated as part of your project.
