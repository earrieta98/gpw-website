From CAD to Cleared Implant

How a medical part moves through GPW.

Step 01 · NDA & Material Sourcing

Confidentiality before files. Spec before chips.

NDA signed before any drawings or 3D models are shared. Material sourced per ASTM F136 (Ti Gr5 ELI), ASTM F138 (316L surgical), or implant-grade PEEK from Invibio or Solvay. ISO 10993 biocompatibility documentation tracked from the mill cert forward.

Step 02 · 5-Axis & Swiss Machining

Implant geometry. Instrument tolerance.

5-axis milling for orthopedic plates with anatomical contours. Swiss turning for bone screws with micron-level concentricity. Wire EDM for surgical blade profiles in hardened stainless — no heat-affected zone, no burrs. The geometry that surgeons depend on.

Step 03 · Electropolish & Passivate

Ra 0.2 µm — the surface that resists bacteria.

Electropolishing for implant-grade surfaces. Passivation per ASTM A967 on every stainless part. Ra measurement per drawing. Cleaning protocols documented and verified. Parts shipped clean — double-bagged when specified, no oils, no chips, no handling marks.

Step 04 · Device History Record

Every part traceable. For the life of the device.

Mill cert. ISO 10993 biocompatibility docs. Process records (machine, operator, date, tooling). 100% inspection on critical features with CMM and GD&T. Ra verification. Cleaning verification. Signed CoC. Complete, accurate, retrievable — not on request.

FAQ

Medical machining — answered.

Are your facilities cleanroom-certified?

Our CNC machining facilities follow medical-grade cleaning protocols — parts are cleaned, inspected, and packaged to prevent contamination. For parts requiring cleanroom assembly or packaging, our EMS division provides controlled-environment capabilities. → See EMS Assembly

Can you machine PEEK for implants?

Yes. We machine implant-grade PEEK (Invibio PEEK-OPTIMA, Solvay KetaSpire) with full lot traceability. PEEK implants require specific tooling strategies, controlled cutting parameters, and careful chip management to avoid surface contamination.

What surface finish can you achieve?

Ra 0.8 µm as-machined. Ra 0.4 µm with finish passes. Ra 0.2 µm via electropolishing. We specify the right finishing process based on your surface requirement and material during DFM review.

Do you handle prototypes and production?

Yes — from 1-piece prototypes for design verification through production runs of 10,000+ units. The process that makes your prototype IS the process that makes your production parts.

How do you protect confidential designs?

NDA signed before any files are shared. Standard on every medical project — we do not ask, we just do it. Design data on secured systems with restricted access.

Can you provide parts for clinical trials?

Yes. Clinical trial quantities (typically 10–100 parts) are machined from certified material with full DHR documentation. Every part traceable to material lot, process, and inspection data — ready for your regulatory submission.
