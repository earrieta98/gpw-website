## SECTION: What Is CNC Milling?

**Label:** The Process

**Definition Block:**

**CNC milling** is a subtractive manufacturing process where rotating cutting tools remove material from a workpiece to create parts with complex shapes, pockets, holes, contours, and precision surfaces. The workpiece is held fixed while the tool moves across multiple axes to cut the programmed geometry.

**H2:** How GPW Handles Your Milling Project

GPW does not just run your file and ship raw parts. We review your design before production starts — checking for features that increase cost without adding function, tolerances that drive unnecessary precision, and geometries that could be simplified without compromising performance.

This is what separates a manufacturer who reviews your work from one who just runs your file. You get parts that are dimensionally correct, properly finished, fully documented, and shipped ready to use.

**When to use 3-axis vs 5-axis:**

**3-axis milling** handles most parts — flat surfaces, pockets, holes, slots, and contours on one plane. It is faster and less expensive per part. If your geometry can be machined from one or two orientations, 3-axis is the right choice.

**5-axis milling** is for parts with complex contours, undercuts, or features on multiple angled surfaces that would require multiple setups on a 3-axis machine. Turbine blades, impellers, aerospace structural fittings, and complex housings typically need 5-axis. Fewer setups means better accuracy and shorter cycle time — but machine time costs more.

Our engineering team recommends the right approach during your DFM review. If 3-axis gets the job done, we will not quote you for 5-axis.

Upload your CAD file (STEP, IGES, DXF, DWG) or drawing (PDF) along with material, quantity, and tolerance requirements. If you are not sure about any of these, tell us — we help.

**Step 02 — DFM Review + Quote**
Our engineering team reviews your design within 24 business hours. You receive a quote with pricing, lead time, and DFM feedback — including specific recommendations to reduce cost or improve manufacturability. No charge for the review.

**Step 03 — First Article Production**
We machine your first article and perform dimensional inspection against your drawing. You receive an inspection report with measured values vs. nominal for every critical dimension. You approve before we proceed.

**Step 04 — Production Run**
Approved parts go into production. Every batch is inspected per your quality requirements — dimensional checks, surface finish verification, material certification.

**Step 05 — Finishing + Shipping**
Parts are finished per your spec (anodized, plated, coated, heat-treated), packaged to prevent damage, and shipped to your dock. Full documentation accompanies every shipment: inspection report, material cert, certificate of conformance.

Standard tolerance is ±0.001" (±0.025mm). We can hold ±0.0005" (±0.013mm) on specified features with appropriate fixturing and inspection. Tolerances are confirmed during DFM review.

No. From 1 piece to 10,000+ per month. We scale with your demand.
