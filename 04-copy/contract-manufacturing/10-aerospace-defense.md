## SECTION: Challenges

**Label:** What Aerospace Demands

**H2:** Aerospace Manufacturing Challenges

Aerospace is the most demanding machining environment. Tolerances are measured in tenths. Materials fight back. Documentation requirements rival the complexity of the parts themselves.

**Tolerances in tenths** — A bracket that holds ±0.005" in industrial equipment holds ±0.0005" in an aircraft. Every feature is critical because weight optimization means there is no excess material to absorb error.

**Exotic materials** — Titanium Grade 5 is half the weight of steel but three times harder to machine. Inconel 718 work-hardens on contact — dull tools make it worse. These materials require experienced operators, rigid setups, and proper tooling strategies.

**Full traceability** — Every part must trace back to the raw material heat lot, the machine it was made on, the operator who ran it, and the inspection data that verified it. This is not optional. It is the baseline.

**Documentation burden** — First Article Inspection reports per AS9102. Material certifications per AMS or ASTM. Certificates of Conformance. Process documentation. Dimensional reports with GD&T callouts. The paperwork is as much a deliverable as the part.

**Qualification cycles** — Getting qualified as an aerospace supplier takes months. But once qualified, programs run for years. The cost of switching suppliers is high enough that OEMs stay with vendors who deliver consistently.

## SECTION: Quality

**H2:** Quality & Compliance

Aerospace quality is not a checkbox — it is a system. Every process, every measurement, and every decision is documented and traceable.

**AS9100D**
Aerospace quality management system. GPW is on the AS9100D pathway — our processes follow AS9100-aligned procedures. We provide the documentation packages that AS9100 requires regardless of certification status.

**ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations)**
Defense-related components require ITAR-registered facilities with controlled access and US-person oversight. GPW operates under controlled access protocols and signs ITAR agreements before receiving defense-related files.

**NADCAP**
Special process accreditation for heat treatment, surface finishing, and non-destructive testing. GPW coordinates NADCAP-accredited processes through qualified partner facilities.

**First Article Inspection (FAI)**
Per AS9102 — a comprehensive dimensional report of the first production part. Every characteristic on the drawing is measured, recorded, and reported against nominal with tolerance. The FAI is your evidence that our process produces conforming parts before we run the production batch.

**Material Certifications**
Mill certifications per AMS or ASTM showing chemical composition, mechanical properties, and heat lot traceability. Certifications accompany every shipment.

**Documentation Package (standard on aerospace projects):**
- First Article Inspection report (AS9102)
- Dimensional inspection report (critical features)
- Material certification (mill cert with heat lot)
- Certificate of Conformance (CoC)
- Process records (machine, operator, date, tooling)
- Surface finish certification (if applicable)

"In aerospace, the paperwork is the product. If you cannot prove the part is correct, it does not matter that it is correct."

**USMCA compliance** — Parts manufactured in Mexico under USMCA qualify as North American origin. This matters for government and defense procurement where country-of-origin requirements apply.

**Supply chain security** — Reducing dependence on Asian suppliers for defense-sensitive components is not just a preference — it is increasingly a requirement. ITAR restrictions limit where defense parts can be made. Mexico is a USMCA partner with a mature manufacturing ecosystem.

## SECTION: FAQ

**H2:** Common Questions About Aerospace CNC Machining

**Q: Do you hold AS9100D certification?**
GPW is pursuing AS9100D certification. Our processes follow AS9100-aligned quality procedures today. We provide full documentation packages — FAI per AS9102, material certifications, CoC, dimensional reports, and process records — on every aerospace project, regardless of certification status.

**Q: Can you machine titanium and Inconel?**
Yes. These materials require experienced operators, rigid machine setups, and proper cutting strategies — slow speeds, high feed, sharp carbide or ceramic tooling, and aggressive coolant. Our partner shops have dedicated setups for exotic alloys.

**Q: How do you handle ITAR-controlled parts?**
We sign NDAs and ITAR agreements before receiving any files or technical data. Controlled access protocols are in place. Contact us to discuss your specific ITAR requirements before submitting files.

**Q: What documentation do you provide?**
Full aerospace documentation package: First Article Inspection (AS9102), material certifications (AMS/ASTM), dimensional inspection reports, Certificate of Conformance, and process records. Scope is defined per project — tell us what your quality system requires.

**Q: Can you handle both prototype and production aerospace parts?**
Yes. From 1-piece prototype for design validation to recurring production runs. Prototype parts are made from the same certified material and inspected to the same standard as production parts.

**Q: What about non-destructive testing (NDT)?**
Through NADCAP-accredited partner labs: fluorescent penetrant inspection (FPI), magnetic particle inspection (MPI), radiographic inspection, and ultrasonic testing. Coordinated as part of your project.
