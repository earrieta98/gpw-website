## SECTION: Challenges

**Label:** What the Field Demands

**H2:** Oil & Gas Manufacturing Challenges

Oil and gas parts do not operate in a lab. They operate a mile underground, submerged in seawater, or inside a pipeline carrying corrosive fluids at 5,000 PSI. When a part fails downhole, you do not send it back for warranty. You mobilize a workover rig at $50,000 per day and hope the fishing tool can retrieve it.

**Extreme environments** — High pressure (15,000+ PSI), high temperature (500°F+), corrosive fluids (H₂S, CO₂, brine, acid), abrasive media (sand-laden flow). Parts must resist all of these — simultaneously — for years without inspection access.

**Material requirements** — Standard steels corrode in weeks downhole. Sour service environments (H₂S) cause sulfide stress cracking in susceptible materials. NACE MR0175 defines which materials survive and which fail. Using a non-compliant material is not a cost decision — it is a safety incident waiting to happen.

**Full material traceability** — The API and NACE frameworks require complete Material Test Reports (MTR): chemical composition, mechanical properties, heat treatment records, and heat lot traceability. If you cannot prove the material is what the MTR says it is, the part is scrap — regardless of how well it was machined.

**Downtime urgency** — A drilling rig costs $25,000–$100,000 per day. A production platform losing $200,000/day in deferred production does not wait 6 weeks for parts from Asia. Hours matter. Days matter. Proximity matters.

**Sealing surfaces** — Downhole tools depend on metal-to-metal seals and elastomer-to-metal interfaces. A scratch on a sealing surface is a leak path. A leak path downhole is a well control event. Surface finish on sealing diameters is a functional requirement measured in microinches.

## SECTION: Materials

**H2:** Materials for Oil & Gas

Material selection in oil and gas is governed by the operating environment — pressure, temperature, and corrosion chemistry. The wrong material does not just fail — it fails catastrophically and expensively.

| Material | O&G Applications | Why This Material |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Stainless 316 / 316L | General corrosive service — valves, fittings, manifolds, instrument tubing | Standard corrosion-resistant grade. NACE compliant in most conditions. |
| Duplex 2205 | Subsea hardware, flowlines, heat exchangers | 2× the strength and 2× the chloride resistance of 316 |
| Super Duplex 2507 | High-chloride subsea, HPHT wells, chemical injection | The step up when Duplex is not enough |
| Inconel 625 | Wellhead components, subsea connectors, exhaust systems | Corrosion + high-temp up to 1800°F. Resists pitting and crevice corrosion in chloride. |
| Inconel 718 | Downhole tools, drill motor rotors, fasteners at depth | High strength (180,000 PSI) at temperatures up to 1300°F |
| Titanium Grade 5 | Lightweight high-strength components, risers, fasteners | Seawater immunity. Half the weight of steel. |
| Steel 4340 | Drill collars, tool joints, subs, high-stress structural | 180,000+ PSI heat-treated. Tough under impact. |
| Steel 4140 | Lower-stress structural, surface equipment, fixtures | Lower cost alternative to 4340 for non-critical components |
| PEEK | Backup rings, seals, insulators, electrical connectors | Chemical resistant to everything downhole. 480°F continuous. Compressive strength for seal applications. |
| PTFE / Teflon | Valve seats, gaskets, seal elements | Chemically inert. Wide temperature range. Lowest friction for dynamic seals. |

**NACE MR0175 compliance:**
All materials for sour service (H₂S environments) are sourced per NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156. This means:
- Maximum hardness limits (typically 22 HRC for carbon/low-alloy steels)
- Specific heat treatment requirements
- Chemical composition within defined limits
- Full MTR documentation proving compliance

GPW verifies NACE compliance at the material sourcing stage — not after machining.

## SECTION: Quality

**H2:** Quality & Compliance

**API Standards**
American Petroleum Institute standards govern dimensions, threading, testing, and material requirements for oilfield equipment. GPW machines per applicable API specs — dimensions to drawing, threads per API 5B, materials per API material groups.

**NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156**
Defines material requirements for equipment in H₂S-containing (sour) environments. We source NACE-compliant materials with MTR documentation proving chemical composition and heat treatment compliance.

**Material Test Reports (MTR)**
Every shipment of oil & gas parts includes MTR documentation:

| MTR Element | What It Shows |
|-------------|---------------|
| Chemical composition | Actual chemistry vs specification limits |
| Mechanical properties | Tensile, yield, elongation, hardness |
| Heat treatment | Process, temperature, time, cooling method |
| Heat lot traceability | Links finished part to specific melt/heat lot |
| NACE compliance statement | Confirms material meets MR0175 requirements |

**Dimensional inspection**
- CMM reporting with GD&T callouts on critical features
- Thread gauging (go/no-go) per API 5B on every threaded component
- Surface finish measurement on sealing surfaces
- Concentricity and runout on rotating components

**Pressure testing**
Hydrostatic and pneumatic testing available through qualified partner facilities when required by your specification.
