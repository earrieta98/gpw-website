## SECTION: What Happens Next

**H2:** What Happens After You Submit

**Step 1 — Engineering Review (within 24 hours)**
Our engineering team reviews your files and requirements. We check geometry, material, tolerances, and process feasibility.

**Step 2 — Quote + DFM Feedback**
You receive a detailed quote with pricing, lead time, and — if you checked the box — a DFM review with specific recommendations to improve manufacturability or reduce cost. We quote the part as-designed AND with our recommended changes, so you can see the difference.

**Step 3 — Your Decision**
Review the quote. Ask questions. Request modifications. When you approve, we produce a first article for your validation before running production.

No pressure. No follow-up calls unless you want them. The quote speaks for itself.

## SECTION: What We Need From You

**H2:** What Helps Us Quote Accurately

The more information you provide, the more accurate your quote will be:

- **CAD file** (STEP preferred) — gives us exact geometry, no ambiguity
- **2D drawing with dimensions and tolerances** — critical for GD&T callouts, surface finish specs, and thread details
- **Material specification** — alloy and temper (e.g., "Aluminum 6061-T6", not just "aluminum")
- **Surface finish requirements** — as-machined, anodized, plated, specific Ra value
- **Quantity and frequency** — one-time order or recurring production? Annual volume estimate helps us optimize pricing.
- **Industry or application context** — tells us which certifications and documentation to include

**Don't have all of this?** Submit what you have. A sketch with dimensions works. A photo of an existing part works. A description of what you need works. We will ask for what is missing — we will not reject your request because it is incomplete.
