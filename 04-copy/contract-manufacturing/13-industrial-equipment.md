## SECTION: Challenges

**Label:** What Industrial Manufacturing Demands

**H2:** Industrial Manufacturing Challenges

Industrial equipment machining is not one type of project — it is every type. One week it is a 500-piece production run of steel shafts. The next it is a single replacement bushing for a machine that has been running since 1995.

**Part variety** — No two projects are the same. Industrial equipment spans conveyors, presses, pumps, compressors, packaging machines, material handling, food processing lines, and everything in between. Each machine has its own drawings, its own materials, its own tolerances.

**Durability** — Industrial parts operate under load, vibration, abrasion, impact, heat, and chemical exposure — often simultaneously. A bearing surface that wears out in 6 months costs more in downtime than the part itself ever cost to make. Material selection and heat treatment are not optional decisions — they are operational ones.

**Legacy replacement parts** — The machine is 20 years old. The OEM no longer makes the part. The drawing is a photocopy of a photocopy. Or there is no drawing at all — just a worn-out part that somebody pulled off the machine. This is a real scenario, and it happens constantly in industrial manufacturing.

**Downtime costs** — When a production line is down, every hour costs thousands. The maintenance manager does not need a vendor who quotes in five days. They need parts — fast.

**Custom fixtures and tooling** — Every production line has custom jigs, fixtures, gauges, and tooling that no catalog sells. These are one-off parts designed for one specific purpose on one specific machine.

## SECTION: What We Make

**H2:** Parts We Machine for Industrial Equipment

Industrial equipment spans too many categories for a short list. Here is what we make most often:

**Rotating components**
Shafts, spindles, rollers, pulleys, couplings, flywheels, impellers. Steel 4140 and 4340 for high-load applications. Aluminum 6061 when weight matters. Turned, balanced, and finished.

**Wear components**
Bushings, bearings, wear plates, slide pads, thrust washers, wear rings. Bronze 932 for self-lubricating bearing surfaces. UHMW for conveyor guides. Delrin for low-friction guides and rollers. These parts wear out by design — having a reliable, cost-effective source for replacements keeps your line running.

**Structural and mounting**
Brackets, mounting plates, base plates, adapter plates, motor mounts, sensor mounts. Steel for heavy loads. Aluminum for lighter assemblies. Sheet metal for enclosures and guards.

**Fixtures, jigs, and tooling**
Weld fixtures, assembly jigs, inspection gauges, go/no-go fixtures, locating pins, dowels, clamps. Custom tooling designed for your specific production process. This is one of our most common industrial project types.

**Hydraulic and pneumatic components**
Manifold blocks, valve bodies, cylinder components, fittings, adapters. Machined from steel, stainless, or aluminum depending on pressure and medium.

**Replacement and legacy parts**
The original supplier is gone. The drawing is lost. The part is worn beyond measurement. Send us what you have — the physical part, a sketch, a photo with dimensions — and we reproduce it. We reverse-engineer, create CAD if needed, and machine a replacement.

**H2:** Why Nearshore Industrial Parts?

**Recurring savings that compound** — Industrial equipment companies reorder the same parts for years. Bushings wear out. Seals fail. Fixtures break. Every repeat order at 60% labor savings adds up over time. A $5,000/year parts spend becomes $2,000/year. A $50,000/year spend becomes $20,000/year.

**Fixture and jig manufacturing at a fraction of U.S. cost** — Custom fixtures, jigs, and gauges are expensive precisely because they are custom. There is no off-the-shelf alternative. Machining them in Mexico at 60% savings turns a $3,000 fixture into a $1,200 fixture — same quality, same material, same tolerances.

**Same-day communication** — When your maintenance team pulls a part off a machine at 8 AM and needs a replacement quote by noon, your GPW contact is already at their desk. Same timezone means same-day response, not next-day.

**Legacy part replacement** — Many U.S. machine shops do not want one-off legacy jobs. They are not profitable enough. GPW handles one-offs at cost levels that make them viable — because labor costs make single-part jobs economically feasible.

**Expedited delivery** — Ground shipping from Monterrey reaches Texas in 1 day, the Midwest in 3 days, and the East Coast in 4 days. For true emergencies, parts can be on a same-day courier.

## SECTION: Reverse Engineering

**H2:** Reverse Engineering — No Drawing? No Problem.

A significant portion of our industrial work starts without a drawing. The part exists — worn, broken, or simply in need of reproduction — but the documentation does not.

**What we need from you:** The physical part, or a sketch with key dimensions, or photos with measurements. Whatever you have.

**What we do:**
1. Measure the part — calipers, CMM, optical measurement as needed
2. Create a CAD model from measurements
3. Send you the drawing for approval
4. Machine the replacement from the approved drawing
5. Inspect the new part against the approved dimensions

**What you keep:** The CAD file and drawing. Now you have documentation for next time. No more tracing photocopies.

This service is included in our engineering support — no separate charge for creating the CAD model when you order the replacement part.
