## SECTION: What We Fabricate

**Label:** The Process

**Definition Block:**

**Sheet metal fabrication** transforms flat metal sheets into functional components through cutting, bending, forming, and joining. Unlike CNC machining — which removes material from a solid block — sheet metal starts with flat stock and shapes it into three-dimensional parts through a sequence of operations.

**H2:** Sheet Metal at GPW

GPW handles the full fabrication sequence — not just cutting. We cut your blanks, bend them to shape, punch the features, weld the joints, install the hardware, and finish the surface. You receive a completed part, not a flat blank that needs three more vendors.

Every project starts with a DFM review. Sheet metal has its own set of design rules — minimum bend radius, bend relief, hole-to-edge distance, grain direction, and K-factor. Our engineering team flags issues before they become expensive problems on the press brake.

## SECTION: Finishing

**H2:** Surface Finishing for Sheet Metal

Sheet metal parts almost always need finishing — for corrosion protection, appearance, or both.

**Powder Coating** — The most common finish for sheet metal. Durable, chip-resistant, UV-stable. Full RAL and Pantone color range. Thickness 0.002"–0.006". Suitable for indoor and outdoor use.

**Wet Paint** — For color-matching requirements, small batches, or multi-color graphics. Less durable than powder coat but more flexible for custom colors.

**Zinc Plating** — Low-cost corrosion protection for steel parts. Clear or yellow chromate conversion. Standard for brackets, fasteners, and internal structural parts.

**Nickel Plating** — Better corrosion resistance than zinc. Also provides EMI shielding when applied to enclosures.

**Anodizing** — For aluminum sheet metal. Type II for corrosion + color, Type III hard coat for wear resistance.

**Passivation** — Chemical cleaning for stainless steel parts. Removes free iron from laser-cut edges, improves corrosion resistance.

**Silk Screening** — Permanent printed labels, logos, control panel markings, and identification on flat surfaces.

## SECTION: Design Tips

**H2:** Sheet Metal Design Tips — Avoid Common Mistakes

These are the issues our DFM review catches most often. Designing with these rules saves time and money:

**Bend relief:** Add relief cuts where a bend meets an edge or another feature. Without relief, the material tears or deforms at the intersection. Rule: relief width ≥ material thickness, relief length ≥ bend radius.

**Hole-to-bend distance:** Holes too close to a bend line distort during forming. Rule: minimum distance from hole edge to bend line = 2.5× material thickness + bend radius.

**Minimum flange length:** A flange shorter than 4× material thickness cannot be formed reliably. Short flanges slip out of the press brake die.

**Grain direction:** Bending perpendicular to the grain is stronger and less likely to crack. Critical on stainless steel and high-strength aluminum.

**Tab and slot:** Use tabs and slots to self-locate parts during welding. Reduces fixturing cost and improves weld accuracy.

**Consistent bend radius:** Use the same inside bend radius throughout the part. Multiple radii require tooling changes, adding cost and time.

## SECTION: Process

**H2:** From Design to Delivery

**Step 01 — Submit Your RFQ**
Upload your flat pattern (DXF) or 3D model (STEP) with material, gauge, quantity, finish, and hardware requirements. 2D drawings with dimensions and tolerances are ideal. If you only have a sketch, we can work with that.

**Step 02 — DFM Review + Quote**
Our engineering team reviews bend feasibility, hole-to-bend distances, grain direction, hardware compatibility, and nesting efficiency. You receive a quote with DFM feedback within 24 business hours.

**Step 03 — First Article**
First article fabricated through the complete sequence: cut → bend → punch → weld → hardware → finish. Inspected against your drawing. You approve before production.

**Step 04 — Production + Inspection**
Production run per approved first article. In-process checks at each operation. Final inspection on dimensions, bend angles, hardware pull-through, and finish quality.

**Step 05 — Finishing + Shipping**
Parts finished (powder coat, plate, paint, anodize), packaged to prevent scratching and denting, and shipped with inspection report and CoC.
