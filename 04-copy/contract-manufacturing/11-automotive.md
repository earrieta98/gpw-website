## SECTION: Challenges

**Label:** What Automotive Demands

**H2:** Automotive Manufacturing Challenges

Automotive procurement is driven by three forces: cost, volume, and documentation. If you cannot hit the target price, you lose the bid. If you cannot scale to volume, you lose the program. If your PPAP package is incomplete, you never get qualified.

**Relentless cost pressure** — OEMs demand annual cost-down targets from every supplier in the chain. Your machined component price this year is your ceiling next year. The supplier who can deliver the same quality at lower cost wins — and keeps winning.

**Volume flexibility** — A program starts with 50 prototypes for validation. Then 500 for pre-production. Then 5,000 per month for production. Then 10,000 when the program ramps. Your machining supplier needs to scale with you — without lead time surprises or quality drift at volume.

**PPAP documentation** — Production Part Approval Process is the automotive industry's gatekeeping system. Without a complete PPAP submission — control plan, FMEA, dimensional results, material certs, MSA, capability studies — you do not ship parts. Period.

**Speed on prototypes** — Design cycles compress every model year. Engineering needs functional prototypes in production materials — not 3D-printed approximations — in days, not weeks.

## SECTION: Quality

**H2:** Quality & Compliance

Automotive quality systems exist because the cost of a failed part at 70 mph is measured in lawsuits and recalls, not rework. GPW builds quality documentation into every project — not as an add-on, but as a deliverable.

**IATF 16949**
The automotive quality management standard. GPW is on the IATF 16949 pathway — our processes follow IATF-aligned procedures. We provide the documentation packages that IATF requires.

**PPAP (Production Part Approval Process)**
GPW provides Level 3 PPAP submissions — the standard level required by most OEMs and Tier 1 suppliers:

| PPAP Element | What GPW Provides |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Control Plan | Process steps, critical characteristics, inspection methods, reaction plans |
| Process FMEA | Failure modes, severity, occurrence, detection, RPN scoring |
| Dimensional Results | All drawing dimensions measured and reported on first article |
| Material Certifications | Mill certs with chemistry and mechanical properties per heat lot |
| MSA (Measurement System Analysis) | Gauge R&R on critical measurement equipment |
| SPC (Statistical Process Control) | Cpk/Ppk capability data on critical dimensions |
| Process Flow Diagram | Step-by-step manufacturing sequence |
| Appearance Approval | Visual standard and surface finish samples |

**Process monitoring:**
- SPC on critical dimensions during production runs
- Control charts maintained per shift
- Out-of-spec conditions trigger immediate containment and root cause analysis

**H2:** Why Mexico for Automotive Parts?

**Mexico IS the automotive hub.** This is not an emerging market for automotive manufacturing — it is the established one.

- **Mexico produced 3.5 million vehicles in 2025** — 7th largest auto producer globally
- **Kia, BMW, GM, Ford, Toyota, Stellantis, Audi, Mercedes-Benz** all manufacture vehicles in Mexico
- **Major Tier 1 suppliers operate here:** Nemak (Monterrey), Metalsa (Monterrey), Martinrea, Magna, Continental, ZF, Bosch
- **Your supply chain is already here** — logistics routes, customs brokers, and carrier networks are established

**Cost advantage:**
- CNC operator: $9/hr in Mexico vs $25/hr in the U.S. = 64% savings
- Material costs: comparable to U.S. (sourced from the same global suppliers)
- Shipping: 1–3 days ground from Monterrey to anywhere in Texas, and 3–5 days to Detroit

**USMCA rules of origin:**
Automotive parts manufactured in Mexico qualify as North American content under USMCA. This is not just a tariff benefit — it is a requirement for many OEM procurement programs that mandate minimum regional content.

**Proximity to your Tier 1:**
If your Tier 1 customer already manufactures in Mexico, sourcing machined components from Mexico reduces your logistics cost, simplifies your supply chain, and aligns your production schedule with theirs.
