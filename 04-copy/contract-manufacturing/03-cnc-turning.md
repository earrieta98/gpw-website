## SECTION: What Is CNC Turning?

**Label:** The Process

**Definition Block:**

**CNC turning** is a machining process where the workpiece rotates against a stationary cutting tool. It produces cylindrical parts — round, concentric, and symmetrical — with high dimensional accuracy and surface finish. When a part is round, turning is almost always faster and more cost-effective than milling it.

**H2:** Turning at GPW

GPW delivers turned parts that are ready to install — not blanks that need secondary work at another shop. We coordinate the full sequence: turning, secondary milling (live tooling), threading, finishing, inspection, and documentation.

Live tooling is available on our CNC lathes. This means flats, cross-holes, keyways, and off-axis features can be machined in the same setup as the turning operation — eliminating a second fixturing, reducing cycle time, and improving concentricity between features.

## SECTION: Materials

**H2:** Materials for CNC Turning

Turning works with the same range of metals and plastics as milling. Some materials turn better than others — machinability affects cycle time, surface finish, and tool life.

**Best machinability (fastest, lowest cost):**

| Material | Why It Turns Well |
|----------|-------------------|
| Aluminum 6061 | Soft, clean chips, excellent surface finish |
| Brass 360 | Best machinability of any common metal, free-cutting |
| Stainless 303 | Free-machining stainless, designed for high-speed turning |
| Delrin / Acetal | Clean chips, tight tolerances, no burrs |
| Steel 1018 | Low carbon, machines smooth, low tool wear |

**Higher performance (slower, higher cost):**

| Material | Why It's Worth It |
|----------|-------------------|
| Stainless 316 | Superior corrosion resistance (medical, marine, chemical) |
| Stainless 17-4PH | High strength + corrosion resistance, heat-treatable |
| Titanium Grade 5 | Strength-to-weight, biocompatible, aerospace-grade |
| Steel 4140 / 4340 | High strength, heat-treatable, demanding applications |
| PEEK | High-performance plastic, heat and chemical resistant |

## SECTION: Finishing

**H2:** Finishing Options for Turned Parts

- **Passivation** — Removes free iron from stainless steel surfaces, improves corrosion resistance. Standard for medical and food-contact parts.
- **Anodizing** — Type II or Type III for aluminum turned parts. Adds corrosion protection and color.
- **Plating** — Nickel, zinc, chrome for steel parts. Corrosion and wear protection.
- **Grinding** — Cylindrical grinding for surface finishes beyond what turning achieves. Ra 0.2 µm or better.
- **Heat Treatment** — Hardening, tempering, case hardening for steel shafts and wear components.
- **Black Oxide** — Minimal dimensional change, dark finish for tooling and fasteners.
- **Laser Marking** — Part numbers, serial numbers, date codes on the OD or face.

## SECTION: Turning vs Milling

**H2:** CNC Turning vs CNC Milling — When to Use Which

| Factor | CNC Turning | CNC Milling |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Part shape | Round / cylindrical | Prismatic / complex 3D |
| Typical parts | Shafts, pins, fittings, bushings | Brackets, housings, plates, manifolds |
| Speed | Faster for round parts | Faster for flat/angular parts |
| Cost | Lower per piece for cylindrical geometry | Lower per piece for prismatic geometry |
| Threading | Internal and external, any form | Possible but slower |
| Multi-axis features | Live tooling (flats, cross-holes) | Native capability (3/4/5-axis) |

**Rule of thumb:** If more than 60% of your part's features are round, turning is the primary process. If your part has complex pockets, contours, or features on multiple planes, milling is the primary process. Many parts use both — turned on the lathe, then milled on a second setup for non-round features.

Need help deciding? Our engineering team recommends the right process during your free DFM review.

## SECTION: FAQ

**H2:** Common Questions About CNC Turning

**Q: Turning vs milling — how do I know which one my part needs?**
If your part is primarily round (shafts, pins, fittings, bushings), turning is the primary process. If it has complex flat or angular features, milling is primary. Many parts combine both. Upload your drawing and we will recommend the right approach.

**Q: Can you combine turning and milling in one setup?**
Yes. Our CNC lathes with live tooling can mill flats, drill cross-holes, cut keyways, and tap threads without removing the part from the chuck. This improves accuracy and reduces cost compared to two separate setups.

**Q: What surface finish can you achieve?**
Ra 0.8 µm standard on turned surfaces. Ra 0.4 µm achievable with finish passes. For mirror-finish requirements below Ra 0.2 µm, we add cylindrical grinding as a secondary operation.

**Q: What thread types do you offer?**
UNC, UNF, metric (M-series), ACME, Buttress, NPT/NPTF, BSP, and custom thread forms. Thread gauging (go/no-go) on every threaded part.

**Q: Lead times?**
2–4 weeks from approval for standard materials and complexity. Expedite available for urgent needs.
