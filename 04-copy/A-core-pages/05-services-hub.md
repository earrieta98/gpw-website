## SECTION 2: What Is Electromechanical Assembly?

**Section Headline:**
Assembly That Combines Mechanical, Electrical, and Cabling into Finished Products

**Copy:**

Electromechanical assembly is the process of integrating mechanical structures, electronic components, cable and wire harnesses, and functional testing into a complete, shipping-ready product. Unlike PCBA fabrication — which produces circuit boards — electromechanical assembly takes those boards and all other components and builds them into the final product that ships to your customer.

GPW specializes in this exact scope. We do not fabricate PCBAs. We do not run SMT lines. What we do is receive your bill of materials — the chassis, the boards, the connectors, the cables, the fasteners, the firmware — and assemble it into a finished product that ships from our Monterrey facility tested, labeled, and packaged to your specifications.

This assembly-first focus is what separates GPW from traditional EMS providers. Every process, every workstation, and every quality gate is designed around one objective: building complex products that meet your specifications the first time.

The scope of electromechanical assembly spans a wide range of complexity. On one end: a cable harness with 20 terminations, continuity-tested and labeled. On the other: a multi-rack AI server system with 500+ cables, dozens of PCBAs, thermal management, firmware loading, and 72-hour burn-in validation. GPW handles both ends of this spectrum — and everything between — using the same disciplined process framework: document the build, control the materials, verify at every station, test to specification.

### Service 1: Box Build Assembly

**What it is:**
Box build assembly is the complete integration of mechanical, electrical, and electromechanical components into a finished enclosure or housing. GPW receives your components — PCBAs, connectors, power supplies, displays, brackets, fasteners — and builds them into a product that is tested, labeled, and ready to ship.

**What GPW delivers:**
- Mechanical assembly: chassis mounting, bracket installation, component alignment
- Electrical integration: PCBA mounting, connector wiring, power supply installation
- Cable routing and management within the enclosure
- Firmware loading and configuration
- Cosmetic inspection and final packaging

**Industries served:** AI server racks, industrial controls, telecom cabinets, medical device enclosures, energy equipment, appliance sub-assemblies.

**Why it matters:**
Box build is the most comprehensive assembly service GPW offers. It consolidates mechanical assembly, electrical integration, cabling, and testing into a single program — eliminating the coordination overhead of managing multiple subcontractors.

### Service 2: Cable & Wire Harness Assembly

**What it is:**
Cable and wire harness assembly is the fabrication and integration of organized cable bundles that connect electrical components within a product. GPW builds harnesses to specification — from simple point-to-point cables to complex multi-branch harnesses with hundreds of terminations.

**What GPW delivers:**
- Custom cable harness fabrication from engineering drawings or samples
- Crimping, soldering, and termination to IPC-A-620 workmanship standards
- Continuity testing and Hi-Pot testing for every harness
- Labeling and identification per customer specifications
- Integration into box builds or system-level assemblies

**Industries served:** All 8 verticals — cable harnesses are a universal component across AI server racks, telecom systems, medical devices, automotive sub-assemblies, aerospace systems, industrial equipment, energy cabinets, and appliances.

**Why it matters:**
Cable harnesses carry power and signal throughout your product. A single faulty crimp or mislabeled connector can shut down an entire system. GPW treats cabling as a precision discipline — not an afterthought squeezed between mechanical and electrical assembly.

[Explore Cable & Harness Assembly →](/electromechanical-assembly/services/cable-harness-assembly/)

### Service 3: System Integration

**What it is:**
System integration is the process of combining multiple sub-assemblies, modules, and components into a complete, functional system. GPW manages the full integration sequence — mechanical structure, component mounting, cable routing, electrical connections, firmware loading, and functional validation — delivering a system that is ready for deployment.

**What GPW delivers:**
- Multi-stage assembly sequencing across mechanical, electrical, and cabling disciplines
- Sub-assembly integration: combining pre-built modules into a larger system
- Firmware loading, software configuration, and system initialization
- End-to-end functional testing and validation
- Packaging and logistics coordination for finished systems

**Industries served:** AI server rack integration (multi-node configurations), telecom system builds, industrial equipment assembly, energy sector cabinet integration, aerospace sub-system assembly.

**Why it matters:**
System integration is where complexity compounds. Each additional sub-assembly multiplies the coordination required — and the opportunities for error. GPW assigns dedicated program managers to system integration projects, ensuring every interface, every cable route, and every test protocol is documented and controlled.

### Service 4: Testing & Inspection

**What it is:**
Testing and inspection encompasses all quality validation activities performed during and after assembly — from in-process inspection gates to end-of-line functional testing, burn-in, and Hi-Pot testing. GPW integrates testing into the production line rather than treating it as a separate post-assembly step.

**What GPW delivers:**
- First article inspection (FAI) on every new program before production ramp
- In-process quality gates at critical assembly stations
- End-of-line functional testing with documented pass/fail criteria
- Burn-in testing for products requiring extended operational validation
- Hi-Pot (high potential) testing for electrical safety compliance
- Full BOM-level component traceability from receiving to shipment

**Industries served:** All 8 verticals — every product GPW assembles passes through testing and inspection. Medical devices, aerospace sub-assemblies, and AI server racks typically require the most rigorous testing protocols.

**Why it matters:**
Testing is not a final checkbox. It is integrated into every stage of GPW's assembly process. Catching a defect at station 3 costs a fraction of catching it at end-of-line — and orders of magnitude less than catching it at the customer's dock.

[Explore Testing & Inspection →](/electromechanical-assembly/services/testing-inspection/)

### Service 5: Enclosure & Cabinet Assembly

**What it is:**
Enclosure and cabinet assembly is the mechanical construction and integration of housings, racks, cabinets, and chassis that protect and organize electronic and electromechanical components. GPW assembles enclosures from sheet metal, extruded aluminum, or customer-supplied housings — and integrates all internal components into a finished unit.

**What GPW delivers:**
- Mechanical assembly of enclosures, racks, and cabinets from customer-supplied or sourced components
- DIN rail mounting, panel wiring, and terminal block installation
- Thermal management integration (fans, heat sinks, airflow routing)
- Door assemblies, cable entry points, and environmental sealing
- Labeling, engraving, and cosmetic finishing per customer specifications

**Industries served:** Telecom cabinets, energy equipment housings, industrial control panels, AI server racks (42U and custom configurations), medical device enclosures, aerospace equipment housings.

**Why it matters:**
The enclosure is what your customer sees and touches. It protects the electronics inside, manages thermal loads, and determines how field technicians access and service the product. GPW treats enclosure assembly as precision work — not just metal bending and bolt tightening.
